Michelle Khoe

For the past 10 years, she has been exploring various social and human development issues (poverty, health, human rights, child protection, civil registration and vital statistics, gender equality and social inclusion, environment and disaster risk reductions) through her contributions at UNICEF, UNDP, UNESCO, The World Bank, national and subnational governments, and non-profit organizations in Indonesia, where she was given an opportunity to visit Western, Central, and Eastern Indonesia on different occasions to unfold stories beyond data. In 2023, she co-authored a Digital Literacy and Inclusion background paper for ASEAN Youth Agenda 2023, in which she was interviewed by staffs at the International Telecommunication Union (ITU) Regional Office for Asia and the Pacific, as well as team leaders from Millenium Challenge Corporation / MCA Indonesia Compact II, to discuss the policy recommendations. Concurrently, she joined United Nations Disability Inclusion and Accessibility Advocate Network in Indonesia and led the Website Accessibility Group. Along with a coordinator from the United Nations Resident Coordinator’s Office (UNRCO) in Indonesia, they had multiple discussions with the head of United Nations (UN) ICT Working Group to incorporate accessibility features in all UN websites. Moreover, she has previously assisted The World Bank Indonesia’s Poverty and Equity Global Practice, in visualizing data with ArcMap and QGIS for Digital Economy team; and spent four years in building SEPAKAT, a web-based application for pro-poor analysis, planning, budgeting, monitoring and evaluation that guides the Government of Indonesia to formulate evidence-based policies at subnational level. In 2017, she was selected to participate in 2017 International Consulting Program in Taiwan and collaborated with individuals across the globe to support micro, small and medium enterprises (MSMEs) in Taiwan in expanding their global market. While conducting market research for Gainwise Technology Co., Ltd. (群睿科技有限公司), a local telecommunication company in Taiwan, she analyzed Gainwise's website to improve its user experience (UX) and user interface (UI) design by working closely with its web development team. As a result, there was a significant improvement in the number of visitors and website ranking, after the website has been revamped.

National Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (CRVS) Portal Development
  • Spearheaded the development of national CRVS portal.
  • Collaborated with web designer and developers in building national CRVS portal.
  • Crafted and enhanced product requirements document (PRD) of national CRVS portal.
  • Ensured the portal was delivered as specified in the PRD and fulfilled the security requirements that was requested by Indonesian Ministry of National Development Planning (Bappenas).
  • Relayed 2 penetration test reports from Bappenas to web developers and actively followed up on the portal refinements.
Data Collection, Analysis, Visualization, and Reporting
  • Engaged with 22 Indonesian Ministries and Government Agencies in soliciting civil registration and vital statistics (CRVS) data under five national strategies, which assessed public service delivery at national and subnational level (provinces and regencies/municipalities).
  • Developed 3 online monitoring and evaluation (M&E) questionnaires in XLSForm (Ona or Kobo), and its user manuals / technical guidelines.
  • Monitored daily data submissions of the M&E questionnaires; and resolved technical issues that were found or reported by respondents.
  • Cleaned and transformed 270 data observations in Python, based on the submissions of three M&E questionnaires and collected data from site/field visits.
  • Prepared data visualizations in Google Data Studio (Looker Studio) for output-based visualizations and Tableau dashboards for national strategy visualizations at provincial and regency/municipal level that were embedded on national CRVS portal in 2021-2023.
  • Supported National Secretariat of CRVS in delivering National Strategy of CRVS annual report that will be submitted to the President of the Republic of Indonesia.
